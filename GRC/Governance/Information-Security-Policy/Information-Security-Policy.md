# Information Security Policy  
Version: 1.0  
Owner: Information Security Governance  
Last Updated: 2025-11-20(YYYY-MM-DD)  

## 1. Purpose  
The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to establish the principles, expectations, and responsibilities necessary to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the organization's information assets. This policy provides the foundation for all supporting standards, procedures, and guidelines within the Information Security Management System (ISMS).

## 2. Scope  
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties who access, store, process, or transmit organizational information or use company-managed systems, networks, or services.

## 3. Policy Statement  
The organization is committed to maintaining a secure environment by implementing appropriate administrative, technical, and physical controls aligned to industry frameworks such as ISO 27001, NIST CSF, and NIST 800-53. All information assets must be protected against unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, and destruction.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities  
### 4.1 Executive Leadership  
- Provide strategic direction and ensure adequate resources for the security program.  
- Approve major security policies and risk decisions.

### 4.2 Information Security Governance  
- Develop, maintain, and enforce security policies, standards, and procedures.  
- Monitor compliance and report on security posture.  
- Conduct risk assessments and recommend control improvements.

### 4.3 System Owners  
- Ensure systems under their responsibility implement required security controls.  
- Maintain accurate system documentation and data classifications.

### 4.4 All Workforce Members  
- Follow all security policies, standards, and procedures.  
- Please report suspected security incidents as soon as possible.  
- Complete required security awareness training.

## 5. Information Security Principles  
### 5.1 Confidentiality  
Information must be protected from unauthorized access or disclosure.

### 5.2 Integrity  
Information must remain accurate, complete, and unaltered except by authorized processes.

### 5.3 Availability  
Information and systems must be accessible to authorized users when needed.

## 6. Control Requirements  
### 6.1 Access Management  
- Access must follow the principle of least privilege.  
- User access must be reviewed regularly.  
- Multi-factor authentication must be used where applicable.

### 6.2 Data Protection  
- Data must be classified according to the Data Classification Standard.  
- Sensitive data must be encrypted in transit and at rest.  
- Data retention and disposal must follow approved schedules.

### 6.3 Asset Management  
- All information assets must be inventoried and assigned an owner.  
- Assets must be tracked throughout their lifecycle.

### 6.4 Security Monitoring  
- Systems must be monitored for suspicious activity.  
- Logs must be retained according to compliance requirements.

### 6.5 Incident Response  
- All incidents must be reported immediately.  
- The Incident Response Plan must be followed for containment, eradication, and recovery.

## 7. Compliance  
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of access or employment. Violations may also result in legal or regulatory consequences.

## 8. Exceptions  
Exceptions to this policy must be documented, risk-assessed, and approved by Information Security Governance and relevant leadership.

## 9. Review and Maintenance  
This policy must be reviewed at least annually or when significant changes occur in business operations, technology, or regulatory requirements.
